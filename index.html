About Me

In a nutshell

I am an advocate for human beings in a world of business and technologies.


How I got here

Before officially becoming a UX Designer, I invested a large portion of my life in self-exploration through theater, mediation and yoga. My inner journey and extensive travels around the world gave me a wide exposure to life and people from different backgrounds and cultures. I deeply understand human beings which helps me to intuitively create what they need.


Extras

  • Ability to wear multiple hats, developer, business analyst product owner.
  • Knack to see the big picture while paying great attention to details.
  • Skill to transform complex business ideas into simple intuitive designs.
  • Passion to explore cutting edge tools that allow seamless integration for developers.

Design tools

My favorite combination of tools is Abstract, Sketch and Invision. Abstract allows me to version and document my design commits while collaborating with different teams. Invision is a great platform to share interactive prototypes with stakeholders and transform vector designs into code to be shared with developers. I also work with Balsamic Mockups to create low fidelity wireframes in the early stages of the design process.

UX Process

Reasearch


I research comparative markets and competitors, interview stakeholders and users to understand business goals and user-needs.

Personas and Scenarios


When the budget allows, defining the target audience with personas and scenarios is essential to determine key features and user flows.

Information Architecture


Choosing the most suitable site-structure, navigation and nomenclature. The user must always know where he is and where he can go.

Wireframes


Whether it's low-fidelity paper or high-fidelity interactive prototypes created with sketch or Balsamic, wireframes are the best way to materialize the client vision and our solution.

Visual Design


I love bringing the magic of colors and fonts to the UI while respecting platform specifications. This is the last layer of design that creates desirability and emotion for the user.

Interactive Prototypes


For testing with users and illustrating our vision to stakeholders, interactive prototypes are a wonderful tool. They simulate the behaviour of the end product.

Usability testing


Testing is the best way to validate conclusions and ensure that the design provides a real solution to the user's goals. We conduct user-testing as often as possible throughout the product's lifecycle.

Quest On Demand, License Management


License management is a module within Quest On Demand that allows administrators within any orginization to view and manage their Office 365 Licenses. The version released in February 2020 is more focused on License reporting with multiple detailed dashboards to help users better understand their license costs and needs.In the process of developping this module, I have:

  • Conducted extensive user and market research,
  • Gathered requirements by working closely with product owners and Microsoft experts,
  • Designed many iterations of low fidelity wireframes,
  • Designed a refined visual design ensuring to follow the application style guide,
  • Conducted usability testing sessions with real clients to reach the final design.

The images below are samples I selected from the current interface and initial low fidelity wireframes. Here is the full InVision interactive prototype. The product can be viewed in action at Quest On Demand




Quest On Demand, Guest User feature


When I first joined the On Demand team, my goal was to create a great experience for the wide range of users that use the product. A key part to this was creating a landing page to direct different visitors to the path that best fit their purposes. Having created a landing page, the next step was to allow guest users to have a glimpse of the product before they can decide if it fits their needs. While designing this feature, I needed to establish some essential UX behaviour across the application, like error handling, loading, empty states and configuration. Quest On Demand, Design System


I had the privilege of leading an extremely talented team of designers to create a design sytem for On Demand using Sketch. This vector system of reusable components became a sketch library that is the basis of every project we work on. The implementation of the design system took approximately one year of collaboration before it became integrated in the product.


TouchPlow iPhone app

TouchPlow is an application that aims to be the Uber for snow plowing services. I Designed the User interface for the application for both iOS and Android platforms. The app is currently available on the app store and was developed by Iversoft Solutions Inc. While designing this application, I learned the nitty-gritty design requirements and specifications of both Apple and Google. Following the platform's design guidelines is essential for the success of any mobile application.
